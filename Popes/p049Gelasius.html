St. Gelasius


Successor 🠖 St. Anastasius II

 St. Gelasius I (492-496) is known to history as the man whose conception of authority dominated Christendom through the whole medieval period; and the early ages show no better evidence of the recognition of papal supremacy than this pope's deeds and writings. Particular significance attaches to the so-called a passage in his letter to the Emperor Anastasius, written in the year 494.
